DEFENCE ACQUISITION PROCEDURE 2020 - SUMMARY

1. INTRODUCTION
The Defence Acquisition Procedure (DAP) 2020 replaces the earlier DPP 2016.
It aims to promote indigenous defence manufacturing under the Aatmanirbhar Bharat initiative.
The procedure governs all capital acquisitions for the Indian Armed Forces.

2. CATEGORIES OF PROCUREMENT
DAP 2020 introduces the following procurement categories:
- Buy (Indian-IDDM): Indigenously Designed, Developed and Manufactured equipment. Minimum 50% indigenous content required.
- Buy (Indian): Indian vendor with minimum 50% indigenous content.
- Buy and Make (Indian): Purchase from Indian vendor followed by domestic production.
- Buy and Make: Purchase from foreign vendor with technology transfer.
- Buy (Global-Manufacture in India): Foreign OEM must manufacture in India.
- Buy (Global): Outright purchase from foreign or Indian vendor.

3. FAST TRACK PROCEDURE (FTP)
The Fast Track Procedure is used for urgent operational requirements of the Armed Forces.
FTP allows procurement within 6 months for equipment already in service globally.
It bypasses several standard steps to reduce procurement timelines.

4. MAKE PROCEDURE
The Make Procedure encourages Indian industry to design and develop defence equipment.
Make-I: Government funded (up to 70% of development cost).
Make-II: Industry funded with assured procurement.
Make-III: Import substitution of components, upgrades, and maintenance tools.

5. OFFSETS POLICY
Offsets apply to Buy (Global) and Buy and Make categories above Rs 2000 crore.
Minimum offset obligation: 30% of contract value.
Offsets can be discharged through direct purchase, FDI, technology transfer, or training.

6. DEFENCE PUBLIC SECTOR UNDERTAKINGS (DPSUs)
HAL, BEL, BEML, BDL, MDL, GSL, GRSE, HSL, MIDHANI, and DPSU OFB are key entities.
DPSUs get preference under Buy (Indian) category.

7. ACCEPTANCE OF NECESSITY (AoN)
AoN is the first formal step in the acquisition process.
Services Headquarters (SHQ) initiates the process with a Statement of Case (SoC).
Defence Acquisition Council (DAC) chaired by the Defence Minister grants AoN.

8. SINGLE VENDOR SITUATIONS
Single vendor situations must be avoided wherever possible.
In exceptional cases, approval of the competent financial authority is required.
Re-tendering is mandatory if only one vendor qualifies after RFP.

9. TIMELINE TARGETS
AoN to RFP issuance: 6 months.
RFP to contract signing: 18-24 months for major platforms.
Repeat orders allowed within 5 years of original contract at same terms.

10. STRATEGIC PARTNERS
The Strategic Partner (SP) model selects Indian private sector companies as long-term partners.
Current SP categories: Submarines, Fighter Aircraft, Helicopters, Armoured Fighting Vehicles.
SP is selected through a separate qualification process before vendor selection.
