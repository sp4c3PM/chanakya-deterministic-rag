INDIA DEFENCE BUDGET — KEY FACTS AND ANALYSIS

OVERALL DEFENCE BUDGET 2023-24
India's defence budget for 2023-24 is Rs 5,93,537.64 crore, representing a 13% increase over the previous year. This is the highest ever allocation in absolute terms. The defence budget constitutes approximately 1.98% of GDP and accounts for 13.18% of total central government expenditure.

CAPITAL VERSUS REVENUE BREAKDOWN
The capital outlay for 2023-24 is Rs 1,62,600 crore, an increase of 6.7% over 2022-23. Capital expenditure covers acquisitions, new construction, and modernisation. Revenue expenditure is Rs 2,70,120 crore, covering pay and allowances, stores, transportation, and maintenance. The capital-to-revenue ratio has been a persistent concern — a higher capital share signals modernisation intent.

MODERNISATION ALLOCATION
Of the capital outlay, Rs 1,07,084 crore is allocated for capital acquisitions (equipment and platforms). This is the key figure that drives procurement pipeline decisions and influences vendor selection. The remaining capital expenditure goes toward construction and other works.

ARMY, NAVY, AND AIR FORCE SHARES
The Indian Army receives Rs 1,87,596 crore (32% of total defence budget). The Indian Air Force receives Rs 57,137 crore for capital acquisition. The Indian Navy receives Rs 52,804 crore for capital acquisition. The IAF has historically received the largest capital acquisition share due to expensive aircraft platforms.

DEFENCE PENSION
Defence pension for 2023-24 is Rs 1,38,205 crore, a significant portion of the total defence expenditure. Pension costs have been rising steadily and now consume a large share of the revenue budget, leaving less for operational expenses.

DRDO ALLOCATION
The Department of Defence R&D, which funds DRDO, receives Rs 23,264 crore in 2023-24. This represents approximately 5.2% of the total defence budget. DRDO allocation has grown at roughly 8% per annum over the past five years.

DEFENCE PUBLIC SECTOR UNDERTAKINGS
HAL (Hindustan Aeronautics Limited) order book stands at over Rs 94,000 crore as of 2023. BEL (Bharat Electronics Limited) revenue target for 2023-24 is Rs 20,000 crore. GRSE (Garden Reach Shipbuilders and Engineers) has an order book of Rs 22,000 crore. The seven DPSUs collectively employ over 80,000 people.

MAKE IN INDIA TARGETS
The government has set a target of Rs 1,75,000 crore in defence production by 2025, up from Rs 1,06,996 crore in 2022-23. Defence exports target is Rs 35,000 crore by 2025. India exported defence equipment worth Rs 15,920 crore in 2022-23, marking a significant increase from Rs 1,521 crore in 2016-17.

NEGATIVE IMPORT LISTS
To boost indigenous procurement, the Ministry of Defence has issued four Positive Indigenisation Lists (PIL) comprising over 400 items that must be procured from Indian vendors only. Additionally, a Negative Import List restricts import of 101 defence items. These lists are updated periodically to include more complex systems.

IDEF AND DIPLOMATIC PROCUREMENT
India's defence imports have been declining as a share of total procurement. India was historically the world's largest arms importer but has moved to second or third position due to Make in India push. Russia, France, and Israel remain key legacy suppliers. The US has emerged as a major supplier post-2008 DTTI agreements.
