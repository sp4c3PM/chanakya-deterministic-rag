DEFENCE RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT ORGANISATION (DRDO) - OVERVIEW

1. MANDATE
DRDO is India's premier defence R&D agency under the Ministry of Defence.
Established in 1958, headquartered in New Delhi.
Mission: Design, develop and lead to production state-of-the-art defence systems.
DRDO has 52 laboratories spread across India covering all defence technology domains.

2. KEY TECHNOLOGY DOMAINS
Aeronautics: Tejas Light Combat Aircraft, Kaveri engine, UAVs.
Missiles: Agni series (ballistic), BrahMos (cruise, joint with Russia), Akash (surface-to-air).
Electronics and Radar: Uttam AESA radar, electronic warfare systems.
Naval Systems: AIP submarine technology, torpedoes, sonar systems.
Land Systems: Arjun Main Battle Tank, Pinaka multi-barrel rocket launcher.
Nuclear and Space: Support to DRDO's strategic programmes.

3. MAJOR PROGRAMMES
Tejas Mark-1A: Series production underway. 83 aircraft ordered by IAF from HAL.
Agni-V: Inter-continental ballistic missile with range over 5000 km. Inducted.
BrahMos: Supersonic cruise missile. Range extended to 450 km. Export orders from Philippines.
Akash-NG: Next generation surface-to-air missile with active radar seeker.
QRSAM: Quick Reaction Surface-to-Air Missile for Army air defence.

4. TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER TO INDUSTRY
DRDO transfers technology to DPSUs and private industry for production.
Transfer of Technology (ToT) agreements are signed with production agencies.
HAL, BEL, BDL are primary ToT recipients.
Private sector ToT is increasing under Aatmanirbhar Bharat.

5. BUDGET AND WORKFORCE
DRDO annual budget: approximately Rs 23,000 crore (2023-24).
Workforce: approximately 30,000 employees including 7,000 scientists.
DRDO contributes roughly 6% of India's total defence budget.

6. CHALLENGES
Long development timelines compared to private sector global benchmarks.
Cost and time overruns on major programmes (Tejas, Arjun).
Brain drain to private sector and foreign companies.
Limited commercialisation of dual-use technologies.
Dependency on foreign components for critical subsystems.
