INDIA'S MAJOR RECENT DEFENCE ACQUISITIONS

1. RAFALE FIGHTER AIRCRAFT (IAF)
Vendor: Dassault Aviation, France.
Contract signed: September 2016.
Quantity: 36 aircraft.
Contract value: approximately Rs 59,000 crore.
Delivery: All 36 aircraft delivered by 2022.
Category: Buy (Global).
Offset obligation: 50% of contract value (~Rs 30,000 crore).
Key offset recipients: HAL, BEL, Thales India.
Status: Fully operational with 17 and 101 Squadrons, IAF.

2. S-400 TRIUMF AIR DEFENCE SYSTEM (IAF)
Vendor: Rosoboronexport, Russia.
Contract signed: October 2018.
Quantity: 5 squadrons.
Contract value: approximately USD 5.43 billion (Rs 40,000 crore approx).
Delivery: Partial. Two squadrons delivered by 2023. Delays due to Ukraine conflict.
Category: Inter-governmental agreement (IGA), not standard DAP process.
Note: US threatened CAATSA sanctions; India invoked sovereign right to procure.

3. P-8I POSEIDON MARITIME PATROL AIRCRAFT (IN)
Vendor: Boeing, USA.
Quantity: 12 aircraft (8 original + 4 follow-on order).
Contract value: approximately USD 3.2 billion.
Delivery: All 12 delivered.
Role: Anti-submarine warfare, maritime reconnaissance.
Category: Foreign Military Sale (FMS) route via US government.

4. APACHE AH-64E ATTACK HELICOPTERS (IAF AND ARMY)
Vendor: Boeing, USA.
IAF quantity: 22 helicopters. Army quantity: 6 helicopters.
Contract value: approximately USD 1.4 billion (IAF) + USD 800 million (Army).
Delivery: Complete.
Category: Foreign Military Sale (FMS).

5. TEJAS MARK-1A (IAF)
Vendor: Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL), India.
Contract signed: February 2021.
Quantity: 83 aircraft.
Contract value: Rs 48,000 crore.
Delivery: Expected to begin 2025, complete by 2031.
Category: Buy (Indian-IDDM).
Indigenous content: Above 60%.
Significance: Largest ever contract with an Indian defence manufacturer.

6. SUBMARINES - PROJECT 75I
Status: AoN granted. SP model to be used.
Quantity: 6 submarines.
Estimated value: Rs 43,000 crore.
SP candidates: L&T, Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders.
OEM candidates: Naval Group (France), ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems (Germany), Daewoo (South Korea).
Key requirement: Air Independent Propulsion (AIP) technology transfer.
Timeline: Contract expected by 2025-26.
