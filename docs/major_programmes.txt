INDIA MAJOR DEFENCE PROGRAMMES AND ACQUISITIONS

FIGHTER AIRCRAFT PROGRAMMES
Rafale: India signed a contract for 36 Rafale multirole fighters with France's Dassault Aviation in September 2016 at Rs 59,000 crore (approximately EUR 7.87 billion). The first aircraft was delivered in 2020 and all 36 were inducted by 2022. India is in advanced talks for 26 more Rafale-M (naval variant) for INS Vikrant at approximately Rs 50,000 crore.

Tejas Mk-1A: HAL's Light Combat Aircraft in its upgraded Mk-1A variant. The IAF placed an initial order for 83 Tejas Mk-1A aircraft at Rs 46,898 crore in February 2021. An additional order for 97 Tejas Mk-1A was approved in 2023, bringing total Tejas Mk-1A orders to 180 aircraft. HAL's Tejas Mk-1 has achieved 59.7% indigenous content by value. Target for Tejas Mk-2 is 70% indigenous content.

MMRCA 2.0 programme seeks 114 medium multirole combat aircraft for IAF. Contenders include F-21 (Lockheed Martin), F/A-18 (Boeing), Eurofighter Typhoon, Rafale, Gripen E, and MiG-35. The deal is expected to be worth over $20 billion and is the largest ever Indian defence acquisition.

HELICOPTER PROGRAMMES
Apache AH-64E: India procured 22 Apache attack helicopters from Boeing at $930 million under a government-to-government deal with the US. Delivery completed in 2020. The Army has also ordered 6 more Apaches.

Chinook CH-47F: 15 Chinook heavy-lift helicopters procured from Boeing at $1.5 billion. All delivered to IAF by 2020. Used for logistics support in high-altitude areas including Ladakh.

Prachand LCH: 15 Light Combat Helicopters developed by HAL were inducted in 2022. A total order of 156 Prachands has been approved for Army and IAF at Rs 45,000 crore. Prachand is the world's highest-altitude combat helicopter with operational ceiling of 6,500 metres.

NAVAL PROGRAMMES
INS Vikrant (IAC-1): India's first indigenous aircraft carrier commissioned on September 2, 2022. Built by Cochin Shipyard Limited at a cost of Rs 20,000 crore. Displacement of 45,000 tonnes, length 262 metres. Carrier can operate MiG-29K fighters and naval helicopters.

Project 75 Submarines: Six Scorpene submarines being built by Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders under technology transfer from Naval Group (France). Total cost approximately Rs 23,562 crore. INS Kalvari was first delivered in 2017; sixth submarine INS Vagsheer delivered in 2024. Indigenous content in Project 75 submarines is approximately 30%.

Project 75I Submarines: Next-generation submarine programme for six advanced submarines with AIP (Air Independent Propulsion). Estimated cost Rs 43,000 crore. Strategic Partner model will be used — L&T and MDL are shortlisted. Indigenous content requirement is 45% for Project 75I.

P-8I Maritime Patrol: India operates 12 Boeing P-8I Neptune long-range maritime patrol aircraft for ASW and surveillance. India was the first export customer for P-8 globally. Six more ordered in 2021.

ARMY PROGRAMMES
Arjun MBT: India's indigenous main battle tank developed by CVRDE and DRDO. Arjun Mk-1A has 72 improvements over Mk-1 including reactive armour, panoramic sight, and improved engine. Army ordered 118 Arjun Mk-1A at Rs 7,523 crore in 2021.

Dhanush Artillery: Indigenous 155mm/45 calibre towed gun developed from Bofors design by OFB/GSQR. 114 guns ordered for Army. Dhanush can fire standard NATO 155mm ammunition and has a range of 36 km with base bleed ammunition.

DRDO KEY MISSILES
Brahmos: Supersonic cruise missile jointly developed by India (DRDO/BrahMos Aerospace) and Russia (NPO Mashinostroyeniya). Speed Mach 2.8, range 290 km extended to 450 km. BrahMos has been exported to Philippines at $375 million for a 3-battery deal — India's largest ever defence export contract.

Agni-V: India's intercontinental ballistic missile with range over 5,000 km. Developed by DRDO. Canister-launch version inducted. Agni-V brings entire China within India's strike range. Uses ring laser gyroscope navigation.

Akash: Indigenous surface-to-air missile system developed by DRDO with range of 25 km. Akash-NG (new generation) has range of 70 km. India approved export of Akash system to friendly nations in 2021.

K-15 Sagarika: India's submarine-launched ballistic missile with range 700 km. Forms the sea-based leg of India's nuclear triad. Operated from Arihant-class nuclear submarines.

INDIGENOUS CONTENT MILESTONES
Tejas Mk-1 indigenous content: 59.7% by value (HAL figure). Target for Tejas Mk-2 is 70%. The Advanced Medium Combat Aircraft (AMCA) programme targets 80% indigenous content by time of production.
