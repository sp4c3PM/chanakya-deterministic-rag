STRATEGIC PARTNER MODEL - INDIAN DEFENCE PROCUREMENT

1. OVERVIEW
The Strategic Partner (SP) model is a key feature of DAP 2020.
It aims to build long-term capability in Indian private sector defence companies.
The SP model applies to high-technology, complex weapons platforms.
Current SP categories: Submarines, Fighter Aircraft, Helicopters, Armoured Fighting Vehicles (AFVs).

2. SELECTION OF STRATEGIC PARTNER
The SP is selected through a two-stage process.
Stage 1: Qualification of Indian companies based on financial, technical, and infrastructure criteria.
Stage 2: Selection of the SP through competition among qualified companies.
Only one SP is selected per category to ensure focus and accountability.
The SP must be an Indian company with majority Indian ownership.

3. ORIGINAL EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURER (OEM) SELECTION
After SP selection, the SP partners with a foreign OEM.
The OEM provides technology transfer and co-production support.
OEM selection criteria include technology offered, price, and transfer of technology depth.
The SP-OEM partnership is the delivery mechanism for the platform.

4. INDIGENOUS CONTENT REQUIREMENTS
SP model mandates higher indigenous content than standard procurement categories.
Submarines: minimum 45% indigenous content at contract signing, rising to 60% over time.
Fighter Aircraft: minimum 50% indigenous content.
The SP is responsible for developing the indigenous supply chain.

5. ROLE OF DPSUs
Defence Public Sector Undertakings (DPSUs) can also participate as Strategic Partners.
However, the model was specifically designed to bring Indian private sector into defence manufacturing.
Private sector SPs compete with DPSUs in some categories.

6. ADVANTAGES OF SP MODEL
Creates long-term, institutionalised defence industrial capability in India.
Reduces dependence on foreign OEMs for maintenance and upgrades.
Builds export potential for Indian defence platforms.
Aligns with Aatmanirbhar Bharat vision.

7. CHALLENGES
Long gestation periods — SP qualification to contract can take 3-5 years.
High capital investment required from SP companies.
Technology transfer depth from OEMs is often contested during negotiations.
Single SP per category creates monopoly risk post-contract.
